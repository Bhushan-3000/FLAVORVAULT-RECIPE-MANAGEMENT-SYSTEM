CHICKEN BIRYANI


Chicken Biryani is a highly aromatic, mouth-watering staple dish that needs no introduction. Because of its endless nuanced flavors, it is perhaps the most comforting meal of Indian cuisine that has been a part of community feasts, family gatherings, and almost every Indian household for ages. Packed with fragrant rice and juicy, tender chicken pieces, it’s a perfect one-pot dinner recipe. 

Non-veg | Main Course | Biryani

4

16-20 mins

Spicy

600 grams Boneless chicken,
1 1/2 cups Basmati rice, 
3 tablespoons Oil, 
1 inch stick Cinnamon, 
2 Bay leaves, 
5-6 Cloves, 
1 teaspoon Cumin seeds, 
3-4 Green cardamoms, 
2 medium Onions sliced, 
3 Green chilies slit, 
1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder, 
2 medium Tomatoes chopped, 
Salt to taste, 
3/4 teaspoon Ginger Garlic paste, 
1/2 teaspoon Red Chilly powder, 
1 cup Thick yogurt, 
3 medium Onions sliced and fried, 
1/2 teaspoon Garam masala powder, 
1 inch piece Ginger cut into thin strips, 
a few Fresh mint leaves torn, 
a few Fresh coriander leaves torn, 
1/2 cup Milk, 
a few drops Rose water, 
4 Eggs boiled and sliced.


Cut chicken in one inch pieces. Boil rice and set aside. Heat oil in a non-stick pan, add cinnamon, bay leaves, cloves, cumin seeds, green cardamoms and sauté for half a minute. Add sliced onions and sauté. Add slit green chillies and chicken cubes and continue to sauté. Add turmeric powder and mix.


Add tomatoes and salt and mix. Add ginger-garlic paste, red chilli powder and yogurt. Mix well. Add half the fried brown onions. Add the boiled rice on top. Sprinkle garam masala powder, ginger strips, mint leaves and coriander leaves. Sprinkle milk, rose water and remaining browned onions.


Cover and cook on low heat for about ten minutes. Garnish with the egg slices and serve hot.

Dairy: Some recipes include yogurt or ghee (clarified butter), which can be an issue for those with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance.

Nuts: Certain variations might include almonds or cashews as garnishing, so people with nut allergies should be cautious.

Spices: Ingredients like cardamom, cumin, or chili powder could trigger reactions in individuals with spice allergies.

Gluten: If the dish is served with naan or other breads containing wheat, this could be a problem for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease.

Eggs: Some biryani recipes might include boiled eggs as an accompaniment, which could cause issues for those allergic to eggs.

Seafood (Cross-Contamination): If the chicken biryani is prepared in a kitchen that handles seafood, there could be a risk of cross-contamination.
